The Port Arthur Massacre

In 1996, Martin Bryant armed himself with a semi-automatic rifle with a 30 round magazine, and set about on a shooting spree killing 35 people in Port Arthur, a town in Tasmania, Australia. He traveled from his home to several different locations where he shot and killed innocent men, women, and children along the way. This is a map of the chronology of that day.

Australia vs US

In response to the 1996 Port Arthur Massacre, Australia swiftly in enacted stricter gun regulations. Among these regulations were a 28-day waiting period for gun purchases, new requirements for gun licenses, a national firearm registry, and the banning of automatic and semiautomatic firearms. By 1998 gun deaths had dropped by 28%, and as of 2015 gun deaths had dropped by 67% compared with 1996 numbers. Meanwhile, gun deaths in the US have hovered at around 10 times Australia's 2015 numbers per capita. The available data for US firearm ownership and death by firearm is extremely lacking when compared to Australia. Much more research is needed.
